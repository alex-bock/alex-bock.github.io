Mental state inference modeling for Parkinson's disease patients

Parkinson's disease has substantial effects on patients' mental health, and it's often difficult for patients to communicate about mental health because of the disease's impact on cognitive and fine motor skills. This summer, I'm collaborating with Tufts Ph.D. candidate Andy Valenti to build software to change that.

Our goal is to develop a robotic agent that can listen to a conversation between a patient and a therapist or caregiver, infer the patient's mental state from the experiences they describe, and act as a real-time advocate for the patient as the conversation progresses.

Right now, we're working on comparing various language processing, machine learning, and data analysis tools to build a pipeline that takes in dialogue and produces emotional evaluations.
